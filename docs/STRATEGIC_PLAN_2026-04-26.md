## 6. Decision framework

Three orthogonal axes. Pick the combination that matches the life you want:

| Axis | Path A | Path B-Lite | Path B |
|---|---|---|---|
| Timeline | No rush | 5-7 year horizon | 7-10 year horizon |
| Lifestyle | Founder-led freedom | Small team, intense | Scale-up, high-pressure |
| Outcome size | $1-3M founder take | $10-30M founder take | $30M+ binary outcome |
| Risk profile | Low / capped upside | Moderate / significant upside | High variance / large upside |

There's no wrong answer. Each path is a real, defensible business. **The wrong answer is committing to one and executing for another.**

## 7. Per-metro sequencing rationale

### Why Atlanta in Y2 (Path B-Lite)?
Atlanta is 6.3M MSA — 4x Nashville, 12x Huntsville. SAM 1,050. It's the largest single TAM expansion available east of Texas. Tornado alley extension means real storm-driven demand. It's also where HailTrace and AccuLynx have entrenched accounts, so first-mover advantage matters: get in before they price-defend.

### Why push DFW to Y4?
DFW is the prize (1,560 SAM, US hail capital) but also the most contested. Need a real team + brand to break through. DFW done badly in Y2-Y3 burns 6-12 months of founder time without a real foothold. Done well in Y4 with team + capital, it becomes the scale lever for Y6-Y10.

### Why Austin Y5 (B-Lite)?
Austin is a smaller TX market (432 SAM) but high storm intensity and high willingness-to-pay (Hill Country roof prices are 30-50% above national average). Y5 entry plays as Texas extension once DFW is establishing itself.

### Why skip Jacksonville in B-Lite?
Florida hurricane demand is real but seasonally concentrated (Jun-Nov). Eavesight's value prop (year-round storm + roof age + owner contact) is stronger in tornado-alley metros than hurricane metros. Save FL for Y6+ once core SE+TX is solid.

### Why no OKC except in Path B?
OKC has extreme hail (US #2 after DFW) and ~1,500 SAM. But it's small market (1.4M MSA), already saturated with storm-chasing competitors, and far from any other Eavesight metro. Only fits as a focused Y5 add in the full-venture-track scenario.

---

## 8. The decision

Three orthogonal choices. None of them is wrong; the wrong move is hybrid execution (e.g., Path B sequencing with Path A resources).

- **If you want freedom and a profitable business:** Path A. Bootstrap. ~$3-5M ARR by Y10. 100% control. Lifestyle business that pays $200-500k/yr to founder by Y3-Y5.
- **If you want to build a real company:** Path B-Lite. Stage capital, plant Atlanta in Y2, grow into DFW in Y4. ~$10M ARR by Y10. Acquisition-class outcome.
- **If you want to be the category leader:** Path B. Full storm-belt land grab. ~$13-15M ARR by Y10. Bigger outcome, higher variance.

Pick the path. Then execute it consistently for 5 years.
