## 6. Capital strategy — investor odds assessment

### Honest odds at current state (solo founder, no paying customers, pre-Stripe)

| Capital target | Source archetype | Odds (next 60 days) | Prerequisites |
|---|---|---|---|
| $25-100k | Friends/family + warm angels | 75-90% | Demo + story |
| $100-300k | Roofing-industry exec angels (former AccuLynx exec, supply-house CEO, regional chain owner) | 40-60% | Working product + 1-3 pilot users + warm intros |
| $250-500k | Pre-seed institutional (Hustle Fund, Long Journey, Boldstart, Bling Capital) | 25-40% | 5-10 paying pilots + deck + clean solo-founder story |
| $500k-1M | PropTech-focused (Fifth Wall PropTech, MetaProp, Camber Creek) | 15-30% | 10-20 paying customers, $10-25k MRR, multi-metro plan |
| $1.5-3M seed | Construction/vertical SaaS funds (Ironspring, BuildGroup, Brick & Mortar, Loup) | 8-15% | $25-50k MRR, repeatable acquisition channel |
| $3-5M+ seed | Tier 2 generalist VCs | 3-8% | $50k+ MRR, 50+ customers, ideally co-founder |
| Series A | Tier 1 (Sequoia/Accel/Founders Fund) | <2% | $1M+ ARR — 12-18 months out minimum |

### What's working against you

- **Solo founder** — ~70% of seed funds have a "no solo founders" rule. Workarounds: (a) recruit a co-founder, (b) target solo-friendly funds (Hustle Fund, Long Journey, Calm Company Fund), (c) use angels.
- **No paying customers yet** — Stripe isn't wired. The single biggest unlock for fundability.
- **Roofer SaaS isn't VC-sexy** — most VCs are AI/dev-tools/fintech-pilled. Funds that get vertical SaaS: Sapphire, Ironspring, Bessemer (later), Insight Partners (later).
- **Pre-Stripe = no MRR chart** — once you have 5 customers paying and a chart growing weekly, you go from "interesting story" to "investable."
- **Established competitors** — AccuLynx, HailTrace own attention. Need a sharp "why won't they crush us?" answer.

### What's working for you

- **Real product in production** — live storm map, 242k properties, 6.6M property-storm joins. Demo > deck.
- **Cohesive vertical thesis** — "replace HailTrace + AccuLynx + Telefi with one tool, priced under each of them."
- **Multi-source proprietary data moat** — roof-age dragnet (assessor scrapes + ACS + permits + KNN) is hard to replicate.
- **TAM is real** — US roofing software market $2-4B. Storm restoration sub-segment $500M+. Competitor exits prove category.
- **Capital efficient** — 80%+ GM at scale, profitable from Y1. Healthier unit econ than most B2B-horizontal pitches.
- **Demonstrated taste + judgment** — architecture choices (PMTiles + H3 + reveal meter + tier-gated entitlements) signal technical credibility.

## 7. Recommended 6-month capital path

### Phase 0 (Now → 30 days): close fundability gaps

- **Wire Stripe end-to-end.** Non-negotiable. Without it, no real revenue narrative.
- **Close 5-10 paying pilots in Huntsville at FULL price.** Not free. Willingness-to-pay > promise-to-consider-paying.
- **Get to $1-3k MRR** (10 pilots × ~$200 ARPU).
- Continue scrape backfill (still ~80k placeholder addresses pending).

### Phase 1 (30-90 days): build the raise

- **Pitch deck** — 10-12 slides: problem, solution, demo screenshots, market, traction chart, GTM, team, financials, raise/use of funds.
- **Investor target list — 30-50 investors:**
  - 10 industry angels (roofing execs, supply-house CEOs)
  - 15 PropTech VCs (Fifth Wall PropTech, MetaProp, Camber Creek)
  - 15 generalist pre-seed (Hustle Fund, Long Journey, Bling, Boldstart)
  - 10 vertical SaaS funds (Ironspring, BuildGroup, Brick & Mortar Ventures)
- **Warm intro pipeline:** LinkedIn for industry execs, founder communities (On Deck / South Park Commons / Indie Hackers / r/SaaS) for VC intros.

### Phase 2 (90-150 days): run the process

- **4-6 week active fundraise** with weekly investor updates ("here's what closed this week").
- Goal: 1-2 lead checks committed by month 5.
- **Realistic raise: $300-750k pre-seed at $4-8M post-money valuation.** ~10-15% dilution.

### Phase 3 (150-180 days): close + deploy

- Wire money. Hire first 1-2 (engineer + part-time SDR or fractional CMO).
- Begin Atlanta data ingestion as the Path B-Lite Y2 bet.

## 8. Three honest decision framings

### Framing 1: "I want freedom and a profitable business"
- **Take Path A.** Bootstrap. Raise nothing or just F&F.
- ~$3-5M ARR by Y10. 100% equity. Zero board.
- Lifestyle business that pays $200-500k/yr to founder by Y3-Y5.
- Risk profile: low. Upside: capped.

### Framing 2: "I want to build a real company"
- **Take Path B-Lite.** Raise $300-500k pre-seed in 6 months, $1.5-2.5M Series A in 18 months.
- ~$8-10M ARR by Y10. 55-65% founder equity.
- Strategic acquisition outcome at $30-60M valuation feasible.
- Risk profile: moderate. Upside: significant ($15-30M founder outcome).

### Framing 3: "I want to be the category leader"
- **Take Path B (full).** Raise institutional seed → Series A → B path.
- $13-15M+ ARR by Y10. 25-40% founder equity.
- Series B or IPO outcome targeted ($80-150M+ valuation).
- Risk profile: high. Upside: large but binary ($30-60M founder outcome OR zero).

## 9. My recommendation

**Don't pursue institutional seed RIGHT NOW.** Math says 75-85% failure odds at current state.

In the next 90 days, do this instead:
1. **Wire Stripe + close 10 paying pilots** — this is the single biggest unlock.
2. **Raise $100-250k from 2-4 angels** — ideally one roofing-industry exec + 2-3 generalist angels. F&F + LinkedIn outreach.
3. **Use that money to hire fractional engineer + CSM** (20 hrs/wk each).
4. **Hit $25-50k MRR by month 12** with Atlanta launch.
5. **THEN raise a real $1-2M seed at month 12-15** from a PropTech/vertical SaaS fund.

This stages the dilution: ~10% to angels at month 6, ~15% to seed at month 15. ~25% total given up over 18 months for ~$1.5-2.5M raised. Founder still owns ~70% at first real fundraise.

**Alternative — going institutional seed cold today — risks either failure or getting offered terms so bad (40%+ equity for $750k) that you'd be better off bootstrapping.**

## 10. The decision you actually need to make

Three orthogonal axes:

1. **Timeline:** lifestyle (no rush) → exit-track (5-7 yrs) → IPO-track (7-10 yrs)
2. **Lifestyle:** founder-led freedom → small team → scale-up intensity
3. **Outcome:** $1-3M founder take (Path A) → $10-30M (Path B-Lite) → $30M+ binary (Path B)

There's no wrong answer. Each path is a real, defensible business. The wrong answer is committing to one and executing for another (e.g., raising VC then trying to run as a lifestyle business — you'll be fired).

Pick the path that matches the life you want. Then execute it consistently.
